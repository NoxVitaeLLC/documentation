# Dancer Policies

---

## Avatar Requirements

The following technical limits apply to all performing dancers:

- **Poly/Tris:** 350,000 maximum (all other optimization stats remain standard)

:::note
Going over one limitation within reason is allowed if all other stats are under the limit.
:::

---

## SFW Standards

Avatars must align with the event's SFW-wear graphics. We do not enforce avatar style (mecha, furry, humanoid, etc.) — however clothing and optimization remain subject to moderation.

- Clothing must be tasteful and appropriate for public display (swimsuit covering minimum).
- Intent, presentation, and coverage should reflect artistic expression, not sexualization.
- Partial exposure such as stylized swimwear or performance attire is permitted, provided explicit anatomy remains covered.
- Common sense and cultural respect apply at all times.

---

## Go-Go Format

### Slots & Stages

- Each team performs for 45 minutes, matching the duration of their assigned slot.
- 2 stages per slot.
- Each stage includes toggleable vertical poles, suitable for all types of dancers.

### Instance Rotation

- Worlds are rotated every 3 DJ slots for the first half of the carnival.
- Teams follow the directions of the Go-Go Logician who manages transitions and backups.

### Backup Teams

- Reserve dancers may step in when a scheduled performer is unavailable.

---

## Performance Expectations

- Both stages must be filled at all times during a set.
- Teams must be in-world and prepared 10 minutes before their set. Delays affect both scheduling and audience immersion.
- Performers must be in good physical and mental condition — properly warmed up, hydrated, and mentally focused.
- Teams are expected to communicate proactively to ensure smooth transitions.
- Each team must appoint a **Team Leader** responsible for communication, punctuality, and readiness of the group.
- Artistic expression is encouraged within event standards.
- Respect the event theme and boundaries at all times.

---

## Emergency Response

- Medical or mental health emergencies take absolute priority.
- Communicate immediately to event staff — safety always comes first.
